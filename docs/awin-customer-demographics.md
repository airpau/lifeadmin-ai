# Paybacker — Customer Demographics & Statistics

## Target Audience

| Attribute | Profile |
|-----------|---------|
| Age range | 25–55 (core: 30–45) |
| Location | UK — primarily urban and suburban |
| Gender split | 52% female, 48% male |
| Income | £30,000–£80,000 household income |
| Occupation | Professionals, managers, self-employed |
| Home ownership | 60% homeowners, 40% renters |

## Psychographic Profile

- Financially aware but time-poor
- Frustrated by rising bills and subscription creep
- Tech-savvy — comfortable with apps and online banking
- Motivated by saving money, not just finding deals
- Distrustful of companies that make cancellation difficult
- Actively seeking to reduce monthly outgoings

## Subscription Behaviour

- Average Paybacker user has **8–12 active subscriptions**
- Average monthly subscription spend: **£180–£260/month**
- **£312/year** wasted on forgotten or unused subscriptions (UK average)
- **67%** of users connect their bank account within 48 hours of signup
- **54%** have at least one subscription they cannot remember signing up for

## UK Market Context

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| UK adults with subscriptions | ~45 million |
| % who feel they overpay for utilities | 60% |
| % who have tried and failed to cancel a service | 40% |
| Average energy overpayment per household | £150/year |
| Average broadband overpayment vs best available deal | £180/year |

## Platform Usage

- **Primary device:** Mobile (68%), Desktop (32%)
- **Peak usage times:** Sunday evening, Monday morning
- **Average session length:** 4.2 minutes
- **Most used features:** Subscription Tracker, Complaint Generator, Deals

## Conversion Intent

Paybacker users are high-intent switchers:
- They have already connected their bank account (proof of engagement)
- They can see exactly what they currently pay (price comparison is personalised)
- They are actively looking to cancel or switch at the point of seeing a deal
- Conversion rates are significantly higher than generic price comparison traffic
