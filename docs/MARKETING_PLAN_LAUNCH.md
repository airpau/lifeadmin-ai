## 2. Guerrilla Marketing — Influencer Strategy

### Micro-influencer Campaign

**Target:** UK personal finance / money-saving influencers on Instagram, TikTok, YouTube

**Budget:** £500-2,000 for initial wave (10-20 micro-influencers)

**Influencer criteria:**
- 5,000-50,000 followers (micro = higher engagement, lower cost)
- UK-based, personal finance niche
- Authentic voice (not overly polished)
- Audience age 25-45

**Where to find them:**
- Instagram: search #moneysavinguk #ukpersonalfinance #budgetinguk
- TikTok: search "uk money saving" "bills hack" "consumer rights"
- YouTube: "how to save money UK" creators
- Approach via DM with free Pro account + commission offer

**Offer to influencers:**
- Free lifetime Pro account
- £50-200 per post (depending on follower count)
- 10% commission on any paid signups via their referral code (PB-XXXXX)
- "Genuine review" brief — let them use it and share honest opinion

**Content brief:**
- Show the bank scan finding their subscriptions (with amounts blurred if needed)
- Generate a complaint letter live on camera
- Show the spending dashboard
- Mention "3 free complaint letters — no credit card needed"
- Include referral link in bio

**Influencer categories to target:**

| Category | Example accounts | Why |
|----------|-----------------|-----|
| Money-saving mums | Budget-focused family accounts | High engagement, trust factor |
| Side hustle / finance bros | UK personal finance educators | Audience wants to save money |
| Travel bloggers | Flight delay content creators | Flight compensation angle |
| Debt-free journey | People sharing debt payoff stories | Debt response letter angle |
| Property/mortgage | First-time buyer accounts | Mortgage comparison angle |

### Viral Content Strategy

**Own channels (free):**

1. **"Did you know" series** (Instagram Reels / TikTok)
   - "Did you know your energy company must refund your credit within 10 working days?"
   - "Did you know if your flight was delayed 3+ hours, you could get £520?"
   - "Did you know debts over 6 years old are legally unenforceable?"
   - Post 3-5x per week, short form, text overlay on dark background

2. **"We scanned and found" series**
   - Show anonymised bank scan results: "32 subscriptions, £6,717/month"
   - Show deal comparison: "Paying £45 for Sky? CommunityFibre is £25"
   - Show complaint letter being generated in real-time

3. **User testimonials** (as users join)
   - "Sarah saved £847 last year with Paybacker"
   - Share anonymised success stories from complaint letters

### Guerrilla Tactics (Zero Budget)

1. **Reddit** — post helpful consumer rights advice in r/UKPersonalFinance, r/LegalAdviceUK (be genuinely helpful, mention Paybacker naturally, not spammy)

2. **MoneySavingExpert Forum** — contribute to threads about complaints, debt, switching bills. Link to relevant /complaints/ SEO pages

3. **Quora/Google Q&A** — answer "how to complain to [company]" questions, link to our SEO pages

4. **Facebook Groups** — join UK money-saving groups, share tips with Paybacker link in profile

5. **Martin Lewis angle** — email MSE editorial team about Paybacker as a tool. If featured, massive traffic.

6. **Local press** — pitch to local newspapers: "Hampshire tech startup helps consumers fight unfair bills with AI"

7. **Product Hunt launch** — schedule for a Tuesday, prepare screenshots, demo video

8. **Twitter/X** — create content around consumer rights, tag relevant accounts (@MartinSLewis, @Ofgem, @Ofcom)
