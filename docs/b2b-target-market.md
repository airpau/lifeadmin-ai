Researched April 2026. Source URLs at the end of each section. UK-only. Companies named are verified UK-operating with customer-facing dispute volume that touches statutes the Paybacker engine cites (CRA 2015, CCA 1974 s.75/s.77/s.78, UK261, Ofcom GC, Ofgem SLC, FCA Handbook / CONC, Limitation Act 1980, Equality Act 2010, Package Travel Regs 2018, ICO/UK GDPR).

Scoring legend:
- Fit 1-5 (5 = textbook fit, dispute volume × statute density × tech sophistication).
- Priority A = obvious immediate fit, named CX/eng leader on LinkedIn, contactable. B = likely fit but needs specific contact discovered. C = peripheral / partner-route only.

## Patterns

What kinds of companies benefit most:

1. **High-volume, lean-CX, statute-dense queues**: neobanks (Monzo, Starling, Cleo), insurtechs (Marshmallow), challenger-energy (E.ON Next, So Energy), digital-first telcos (Sky, Virgin O2). 15-25 agents handling what 200-agent legacies handle. Per-ticket statute lookup is a clear bottleneck.

2. **Companies sitting on regulatory pressure waves**: BNPL post-FCA-regulation (Klarna, Zilch), motor-finance redress (Moneybarn, Hastings, Admiral), DMCCA subscription rules (PureGym, HelloFresh), April-2026 broadband rises (BT/EE/Sky/Vodafone). Sell the calendar, not the API.

3. **Claims-management businesses where the entitlement engine IS the product**: Bott & Co, AirHelp UK, Tully — the engine moves from CX-tooling to core claim-eligibility scoring.

4. **AI-agent platforms whose biggest commercial blocker is hallucinated UK statute citations**: Sierra, Decagon, Forethought, Cleo, Plum, PolyAI, DigitalGenius. Anti-hallucination ground-truth API wins by deterministic citation. This is the white-label motion.

5. **Consumer-rights aggregators with big intake volume and no entitlement engine of their own**: Resolver, Which?, MoneyHelper, Trustpilot. Partnership > sale, but volume is enormous.

6. **Lookalikes worth chasing that the brief missed**: Klarna/Zilch (BNPL post-regulation), Lowell/Cabot (debt purchasers receive CCA s.77/78 requests at scale), Very Group/Studio (CRA + CCA combo retailers), Moneybarn (active FCA motor-finance redress), Bloom & Wild + AO.com (CRA delivery + faulty-goods volume), PolyAI + DigitalGenius (UK-based AI CX vendors that need ground-truth UK law).

The strongest single archetype: **digital-first UK consumer brand, FCA/Ofcom/Ofgem-regulated, lean CX team, has an LLM somewhere in the agent stack, leadership writes publicly about Consumer Duty / Customer Outcomes**. That's where calls convert.

## Outreach Plan: First 30 Days

**Definition of "qualified" signup**: a paid pilot agreement OR a live integration where the team has hit `POST /v1/disputes` from a non-localhost environment with a real ticket payload at least 50 times. 10 of these in 30 days = green-light.

### Daily cadence

- **Mon-Fri, every day:** 5 LinkedIn DMs/day from priority-A list — focused on CX leaders, Heads of Disputes, Heads of Customer Operations, VP Engineering at AI-CX vendors. Target reply within 4 working hours.
- **Mon-Fri, every day:** 10 personalised emails/day from priority-B list — energy & broadband enterprise sales-ops, BPO partner managers, Zendesk/Freshworks marketplace managers.
- **Tue + Thu, weekly:** 1 community post — Show HN, /r/UKpersonalfinance for awareness, /r/devops for the AI-builder angle, indiehackers + LinkedIn long-form.
- **Wed, weekly:** 2 partner intros sought via warm network into Resolver, Bott & Co, MaPS.

Total weekly volume target: 25 DMs + 50 emails + 2 community posts + 2 partner-intros sought.

### Conversion measure & kill criteria

Tracker: signups by sector × stage (DM → reply → call → trial key → paid pilot/integration). Update daily.

- **Green-light rule:** ≥10 qualified UK signups (paid pilot OR live integration ≥50 real calls) in 30 days = founder commits to deeper B2B build.
- **Sector-level kill:** if a sector has ≥20 outbound touches with 0 reply by end of week 2, pause that sector for the remainder of the 30 days and reallocate volume to the highest-replying sector.
- **Channel-level kill:** if email reply-rate <2% by end of week 1, switch to LinkedIn-only for that sector.

### Weekly review schedule

- **Mon 9am, 30 min:** review prior-week funnel; lock channel/sector kill decisions. Outputs: revised target list, revised message hook, named "person of the week" referrals to chase.
- **Wed 4pm, 15 min:** mid-week pulse on reply-rates + any reg-news angle (Ofgem / Ofcom / FCA / CMA publications) to reflect into outbound.
- **Fri 5pm, 15 min:** count qualified signups towards the 10. Snapshot to founder.
- **Day 14 (mid-cycle):** if <3 qualified signups, escalate to founder for hook revision, not just channel revision.
- **Day 30:** decision meeting. 10+ qualified signups → green-light B2B deepening. <10 → either extend 14 days with revised top-3 sectors OR revert to consumer-side roadmap per founder rule.
