# UGC Scripts — A, B, C

Three loose scripts sent alongside the UGC creator brief. Creators are asked to pick one, treat it as a starting point, and tell the story in their own words. Verbatim delivery kills the "real" feel.

Each script = 30-60 seconds. First 2 seconds = hook. Last 3 seconds = URL on screen.

---

## Script A — The Parking Fine

*For: angry-consumer creators, TikTok especially. The classic relatable problem. Works best if creator has a real parking-fine story or can film at an actual car park.*

**[0:00-0:02 HOOK]**
*Camera on the creator, outside, mid-pace.*
"I got this £100 parking fine for staying in a car park for TEN MINUTES over my ticket."

**[0:02-0:10 THE PROBLEM]**
*B-roll of the fine itself if possible.*
"Private car park. The sign was tiny. And if I just paid it — which is what they want you to do — that's £100 gone. If I tried to fight it I'd have to read a bunch of legal jargon I don't have time for."

**[0:10-0:25 THE DISCOVERY]**
*Cut to phone screen-recording. Creator types problem into paybacker.co.uk.*
"Until a mate sent me this thing called Paybacker. It's a website — you type in what happened, and it literally writes you a legal complaint letter in about 30 seconds. Cites the actual UK law — POFA 2012, all of it. Not me, the AI."

**[0:25-0:45 THE RESULT]**
*Hold up phone showing the generated letter.*
"Look at this. It's like four pages. It's properly written. It sounds like a solicitor wrote it. I just emailed it to the parking company and [insert real outcome — 'they cancelled it within a week' / 'they've reduced it' / 'still waiting to hear'] — all for free."

**[0:45-0:58 CALL TO ACTION]**
*Face back to camera.*
"If you're getting ripped off by a parking fine, a broadband hike, an energy bill — whatever — just try it. Free to use. paybacker.co.uk. Literally took me 30 seconds."

**[0:58-1:00 END SCREEN]**
*paybacker.co.uk on screen, big.*

## Script B — The Mid-Contract Hike

*For: personal-finance creators, money-saving creators. Slightly more educational. Works well as an Instagram Reel + TikTok.*

**[0:00-0:02 HOOK]**
*Creator holding up a letter or phone showing a text message.*
"My broadband just went up by £9 a month mid-contract. Did you know this is technically illegal now?"

**[0:02-0:10 THE HOOK, EXPANDED]**
"Ofcom changed the rules in April 2025. Providers have to tell you exactly — in pounds, not percentages — how much they're going to raise your bill. If they didn't, you can leave your contract for free. And most people don't know."

**[0:10-0:25 THE TOOL]**
*Screen-recording of paybacker.co.uk.*
"Paybacker.co.uk — it's a UK AI tool that writes you the complaint letter, cites the actual Ofcom rule, and tells you how to get out of the contract. 30 seconds. Free to use."

**[0:25-0:40 THE LETTER]**
*Scroll through the generated letter.*
"This is what it writes you. It's formal, it quotes the legislation, it includes the line you need — 'I am exercising my right to exit without penalty under Ofcom's amended Condition C1.' You just copy it, send it to your provider, done."

**[0:40-0:58 OUTCOME + CTA]**
"I sent mine two weeks ago. Switched to a cheaper provider, no exit fee. Saved about £280 over the year. If you've had a mid-contract letter in the last six months, check it — then go to paybacker.co.uk."

**[0:58-1:00 END SCREEN]**

## Script C — The Founder Story

*For: personal-brand creators, tech creators, creators who post more talk-to-camera explainer content. The Paybacker 60-second founder story delivered authentically. Works particularly well on Instagram Reels and LinkedIn.*

*Note: this is the Founder Story 60-second version (see founder-story-three-lengths.md) adapted for UGC delivery — a creator telling a founder's story on his behalf. Use sparingly — it works for the right creator but sounds forced from the wrong one.*

**[0:00-0:03 HOOK]**
*Direct to camera, slightly exasperated tone.*
"I just found out about this UK guy who got ripped off three times in a year and built an AI to fight back."

**[0:03-0:20 THE STORY]**
"Paul Airey. Ex-Freshfields, ex-Norton Rose — spent ten years building systems for massive law firms. Was on the wrong end of a mid-contract broadband hike, a dodgy parking fine, and a mis-billed energy account. Each one, individually, not worth a solicitor. But together, about a grand."

**[0:20-0:35 THE PRODUCT]**
*Screen-recording of paybacker.co.uk.*
"So he built Paybacker. It's an AI that reads every relevant UK consumer law — Consumer Rights Act, Ofgem codes, UK261 for flights — and drafts you a formal complaint letter in 30 seconds. For free."

**[0:35-0:50 THE WHY]**
*Back to direct camera.*
"The point: it's now as easy for a normal person to fight back as it is for a company to rip them off. That's the whole thing. Free tier is 3 letters a month forever, no card."

**[0:50-0:60 CTA]**
"paybacker.co.uk. Go try it on something you've been putting off fighting."

## Script variations / do-your-own-story encouragement

If the creator has a **real** consumer dispute — a bill they're currently arguing with, a parking fine on their desk, a gym membership they can't cancel — the single best video is them filming themselves using Paybacker for that actual thing, unscripted, in one take. Those videos outperform polished scripts by 3-5x on engagement.

If the creator asks for alternative angles:

- **The Forgotten Subscription:** "I just realised I've been paying £14.99 a month for two years for a service I forgot existed."
- **The Flight Delay:** "My flight was 4 hours late and the airline told me I wasn't entitled to anything. This took 30 seconds to prove they were wrong."
- **The Energy Back-Bill:** "My energy company just hit me with an £840 back-bill going back 18 months. Turns out they can't legally do that."
- **The Council Tax Challenge:** "My band is E. My neighbours are all D. This got me a £1,200 refund."

Every one of these is a real UK consumer-rights angle with a specific piece of legislation behind it. The video is the proof.
