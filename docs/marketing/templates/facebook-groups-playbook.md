# Facebook Groups — The Value-First Playbook

**Why Facebook Groups matter for Paybacker:** UK consumer-rights, personal-finance, and cost-of-living groups have the exact Paybacker ICP and they're far less saturated than Reddit. Many groups have 10k-100k members with engaged daily posters. A single useful answer can produce 50+ signups with zero cost.

**Critical constraint:** Most relevant groups ban promotional posts on sight. The only approach that works is **answering real questions helpfully, without a link, until a thread organically asks "how did you do this" — then you answer with the URL.**

## Principles

- **Never post first, always answer.** New threads from a stranger get deleted. Replies to existing threads survive.
- **Answer without the link first time.** Teach them the consumer right. Mention the tool only if asked, or in a second reply.
- **Paul answers as Paul** — personal profile, not a page. Identify as founder clearly in profile bio, not in every comment. People smell corporate shilling; they respect a helpful founder who was there to help first.
- **One group per posting session.** Don't spray. Be known in 3-5 groups over 6 months rather than skim-posting in 30.
- **When in doubt, ask a group admin.** Some groups allow founder AMAs or "Founders Friday" style posts if you ask nicely.

## Comment templates — what a good Paybacker answer looks like

### Scenario 1: Someone posts "Sky hiked my broadband mid-contract, can I do anything?"

**Bad (delete-worthy):**
> "Use Paybacker, it'll help! paybacker.co.uk"

**Good:**
> "Yeah, since April 2025 Ofcom rules require mid-contract rises to be notified in pounds, not percentages. If your letter said 'CPI+3.9%' or similar, it's technically non-compliant and you can exit without paying a penalty. You'd send Sky a formal complaint citing the amended Ofcom Condition C1, give them 8 weeks to resolve, then escalate to CISAS if they don't. The key phrase is 'I am exercising my right to exit without penalty under the amended General Conditions.' Best of luck."

*What works:* actual law cited, specific action steps, no product pitch. If someone replies "how did you draft that" — that's the organic signal to mention Paybacker.

### Scenario 2: Someone posts "Got a £100 parking fine from a private car park, is it enforceable?"

**Good:**
> "Depends — was it a proper Notice to Keeper from the registered keeper (you), or a Notice to Driver handed to someone? Under POFA 2012 Schedule 4, the operator has to follow a specific process — if they got it wrong, the charge isn't enforceable. About 87% of formally-appealed private parking charges get cancelled. You want to appeal in writing within 28 days, cite POFA 2012, and note any specific procedural failures (e.g. was the signage at the entrance legible? GPEOL — 'genuine pre-estimate of loss' — or ParkingEye v Beavis principles). Don't just pay it."

### Scenario 3: Someone asks "what's this Paybacker thing someone mentioned?"

Now you can answer:

> "I'm actually the founder — Paul. It's an AI tool that drafts formal complaint letters citing the exact UK law in about 30 seconds. Free tier is 3 letters a month forever. FCA authorised, ICO registered. Happy to answer any questions — and genuinely, if the tool doesn't work for your case, I'd rather you told me than said yes out of politeness."

*What works:* identify as founder clearly, include the 'free tier forever' specific, caveat that it might not fit. Authority + humility.

## Moderator relationships

Every group Paul becomes active in — DM the mod within the first month:

> Hi [admin name], Paul here — I've been answering a few questions in the group recently on parking / broadband / energy issues. I run a UK AI tool called Paybacker that helps with this kind of thing. Want to flag it so you know I'm not a drive-by spammer. Happy to abide by whatever group rules you prefer — and if you ever want me to do a Q&A for members or answer a specific member's issue as a case study, just say.

Most mods will reply positively. Some will lock you out of promoting entirely, which is also fine — keep being useful in their group. Some will offer a pinned AMA post once per quarter. Take those every time.

## Weekly cadence

- **Mon/Wed/Fri 7-8pm (UK prime time):** 30-min session. Read top 20 unanswered questions across 3-5 active groups. Reply to 5-8 with genuinely useful answers. Never include product link unless asked.
- **Track in `community_engagement` table:** group_name, post_url, reply_text, replies_received, signups_attributed (via UTM when link eventually given), sentiment.

## Automation limits

This CANNOT be automated. Facebook detects templated replies, and group admins delete them faster than any human. The tool available here is Cowork — at most, Claude reads the group's recent posts (you paste them), drafts a reply, Paul personalises and posts manually.

## DO NOT do

- Don't post identical answers across groups (detection)
- Don't link paybacker.co.uk in your bio across all groups (looks corporate)
- Don't create secondary accounts or fake personas (banned if caught; catastrophic reputation hit)
- Don't screenshot and cross-post others' questions to the Paybacker page (breach of trust)
- Don't friend-request people who post problems (creepy)
