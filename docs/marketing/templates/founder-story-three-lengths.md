# Founder Story — Three Lengths

The emotional backbone of every piece of Paybacker communication. Use the right length for the context. **Do not** polish these further — the edges are the point.

## Core thesis (memorise this)

"I'm tired of people — including myself — being ripped off by companies, where normal working people can't realistically pay solicitors to represent them for the day-to-day injustices that happen constantly. This needs to change. That's why I built Paybacker."

---

## A. The one-liner (12 words)

*Twitter bio, email signature, networking intros, first line of a pitch.*

> Built Paybacker because normal people shouldn't need a solicitor to fight a £40 parking fine or a mid-contract price rise.

---

## B. The 60-second version

*Podcast intros, TikTok founder videos, press calls, the "tell me about you" moment in an interview.*

> "I'd been ripped off three times in a year — a mid-contract broadband hike, a dodgy parking ticket, and a mis-billed energy account. Each one I could have fought if I'd had the time and knew the exact law to cite. Nobody does. Nobody's going to pay a solicitor £200 for a £40 dispute. So companies do this on purpose, because they know you won't fight back.
>
> I've got a computer science background and spent a decade building systems for law firms like Freshfields and Norton Rose. I knew the technology to fix this was finally good enough. So I built Paybacker — an AI that reads every relevant UK consumer law and drafts you a formal complaint letter in 30 seconds. Free to try, no solicitor needed. The goal is simple: make it as easy for a normal person to fight back as it is for a company to rip them off."

---

## C. The 200-word version (About page, PR pitch, longer interviews)

*This IS the paybacker.co.uk/about copy. Paste it in. If the existing About page is more corporate than this, replace it.*

> Paybacker started with a feeling most UK consumers know well: the quiet, grinding anger of being ripped off and knowing you probably won't do anything about it.
>
> A mid-contract price rise your provider hopes you won't notice. A parking fine from a car park that knows 90% of people just pay it. An energy bill with a strange "adjustment" you can't make sense of. Each one, individually, is too small to hire a solicitor for. Collectively, it costs UK households billions every year.
>
> UK law is actually on your side. The Consumer Rights Act 2015, Ofgem's billing codes, UK261 for flight delays — the tools to fight back exist. The problem is that normal working people don't have the time, the legal knowledge, or the money to use them.
>
> Paybacker was built to close that gap. Our AI reads every relevant piece of UK consumer law and drafts a formal complaint letter in 30 seconds, citing the exact legislation that applies to your situation. The free tier gives you three letters a month, forever. No solicitor needed.
>
> This has to change. That's why we built it.

## Tone rules — keep vs cut

**KEEP**
- Anger, directed at the system, not at customers.
- Specificity — parking fines, mid-contract hikes, energy bills. Concrete, not abstract.
- First-person honesty — "I was ripped off too".
- The "normal working people can't afford solicitors" framing. Don't soften to "everyday consumers" or "UK households" in spoken content.

**CUT**
- Corporate mission-statement language — "empowering consumers", "democratising legal access". It sounds like every fintech deck.
- Over-hedging — don't say "we believe this might help some consumers". Say "this has to change".
- Tech-first language — never lead with "AI-powered" or "LLM-based". Lead with the injustice. Mention the tech as the solution.
