## 2. Strategic Brief & Discovery

Challenges they identified:
1. Transition from local/business marketing to national tech-first launch with no internal marketing infrastructure.
2. Fintech cold-start problem — building trust in an AI-powered 'Pocket Agent'.
3. Unproven high-intent niche channels (Reddit).
4. Unit-economic balance between free-tier growth and API cost of premium features.

Goals extracted:
1. Execute a high-impact national launch in May 2026, establishing Paybacker as a UK household name for consumer rights.
2. Drive significant free-tier signups to build a remarketing funnel for Essentials/Pro.
3. Achieve dominant search authority; position Paybacker as the primary alternative to Emma.
4. Scale native iOS/Android apps via performance tactics including Apple Search Ads. *(Excluded from in-house plan — no app yet.)*

- UK fintech shifting toward automated financial advocacy as cost-of-living pressures grow.

## 4. Personas

**The Savvy Optimizer** — young professional, UKPersonalFinance-type. Frustrated by manual subscription tracking and utility disputes. Wants to automate admin and never overpay.

**The Household Defender** — busy parent juggling household bills/contracts. Overwhelmed by subscription creep; no time/legal confidence to fight back. Wants a Pocket Agent personal advocate.

## 5. Website & Competitive Audit

### Website issues identified
1. **Banner stacking** — two competing banners at the top. Merge the trial offer and the scarcity message into one bar.
2. **Headline impact** — current headline is generic. Lead with a tangible result, e.g. "Stop Unfair Charges" or "Reclaim Your Money".
3. **CTA friction** — "No card required" text is a masterclass, keep it.
4. **Trust signals** — badges too small. Make more prominent.
5. **Product context** — Telegram mockup is the strongest visual; showcase it.
6. **Mobile stacking** — four feature boxes will stack vertically on mobile, keep icons legible.

### Competitive landscape
- **Snoop** — highlights 4.00% AER. Opportunity: win on a more singular, streamlined focus.
- **Emma** — hero shows breadth (rent reporting, net worth, round-ups). Opportunity: more proactive consumer-rights features (switching, legal advocacy).

### Competitor paid ads analysis
- **Dow Janes (Meta)** — value-first lead magnet ("Free Money Class For Women+, By Women"). Beat by offering an immediate financial win via consumer rights tools.
- **Female Invest (Meta)** — webinar with Barbara Corcoran — authority/social proof. Beat by positioning automation as a more efficient alternative to manual learning.
- **Emma (Meta video, 298 days live, 12s)** — brand storytelling + seasonal relevance. Beat with high-energy, performance-led creative hitting specific pain points.
- **Sparheldin (Meta video, 1 day live, 43s)** — community/empowerment hook. Beat by demonstrating the essential infrastructure they need.

## 8. Middle-of-Funnel — Nurture & Conversion

### Automated email flows (4)
1. **First Win Onboarding** — link first account via Open Banking + generate first AI dispute letter within 48h.
2. **Subscription Recovery** — triggered on high-cost/redundant sub detection → cancellation tools prompt.
3. **Tier Migration** — nurture free users toward Essentials/Pro with advanced protections/automation.
4. **In-App Milestone Celebration** — fired on dispute resolved or savings milestone; invite to advocate.

### Recurring campaigns (2)
1. **Monthly Bill Shock Report** — spending analysis surfacing where money went; position Pro features.
2. **Consumer Rights Spotlight** — educational content around UK news (energy price changes, rail fare hikes) linking to Paybacker tools.

### Segmentation
By engagement level, dispute history, spending patterns. Hyper-relevant content for high-sub vs one-off dispute users.

### Retargeting
- Categorise audience by interaction with AI tools.
- Tailored social proof across Meta and Reddit.
- Specific plays:
  1. Move passive observers → free members by highlighting immediate financial relief from letters.
  2. For downloads who won't link accounts → video explaining Open Banking security.
  3. ASA retargeting for App Store interactions. *(excluded in-house)*
- Objective: reduce Time-to-Value; accelerate free → paid.

## 9. Creative Strategy

Core thesis: "In a saturated fintech market, your creative is no longer just a visual asset; it is the primary engine of your growth."

Stats cited:
- 80% of campaign performance is determined by creative quality, not targeting.
- 3-5x ROI improvement from systematically testing creative variants.
- 50% reduction in CPA when creative is optimised for platform and audience.

## 10. Four Creative Routes (15–30s vertical video)

### Route 1 — The AI Legal Eagle
- Hook: "How I got £140 back from my water company in 5 minutes."
- Style: handheld iPhone, GRWM, natural window light.
- 5 scenes: disbelief → frustration → dispute letter gen → success email → bold centre-screen captions (yellow/white).
- Talent: 30-45 UK pro/HoH, casual-smart.

### Route 2 — The Subscription Audit Walkthrough
- Hook: "You are probably losing £30 a month on subscriptions you don't even use."
- Style: PIP creator + app UI (green-screen on TikTok/Reels).
- 4 scenes: stop-hand gesture → Money Hub auto-identify → one-tap cancel → savings + bank-link CTA.
- Captions: white-on-black highlight, top or bottom.
- Talent: 22-30, energetic, life-hack type.

### Route 3 — The Pocket Agent POV
- Hook: "Asking my AI agent if I can afford this holiday."
- Style: POV camera, in transit (bus/train/high street).
- 4 scenes: type question → instant Pocket Agent reply → creator explanation → app icon + Link-in-Bio.
- Captions: chat-bubble style in brand colours.
- Talent: 25-35 urban professional.

### Route 4 — The Side-by-Side Bill Battle
- Hook: silent 2-second frustration vs calm split, "Stop fighting your bills alone."
- Style: split screen, tripod, consistent framing.
- 4 scenes: manual vs app → phone queue vs toggle → screens merge with explanation → smile + App Store graphic.
- Captions: THEM/YOU or OLD/NEW in high-contrast red/green.
- Talent: 30-50 UK homeowner, trustworthy.

## 13. UGC Production — Performance UGC at Scale

Rigorous scientific process. Sprints every 2–4 weeks; compounding creative advantage.

Stats cited:
- 93% of creative fatigue offset by weekly rotating UGC.
- 4x higher CTR vs studio on Meta/TikTok.
- 50% lower CPA with systematic A/B UGC testing.
- 79% of consumers say UGC impacts purchase decisions.

### 5-step cycle
1. **Data & Insight Mining** — analyse own ads, competitor UGC, trending formats.
2. **Creative Briefing & Scripting** — shot lists, variations, hooks, talent direction.
3. **Creator Casting & Production** — vetted network; guided not over-directed.
4. **Edit, Caption & Format** — 3–5 variants per shoot, native per platform.
5. **Test, Learn & Scale** — hook retention, thumb-stop, CTR, CPA; kill losers fast.
